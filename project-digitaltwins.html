Summer internship

Digital Twins in Radiotherapy: Beam Comparison & Plan Optimisation

Role: Summer InternWhere: Centre François BaclesseSupervisors: Prof. Juliette Thariat, Dr. Thao Nguyen PhamStack: MatRad, DICOM, DVH

Patient-specific MatRad simulations comparing photon, proton, helium and carbon-ion plans — quantifying which modality best spares vision-critical organs while covering the tumour.

The problem

The central trade-off in radiotherapy is coverage versus collateral: deliver enough dose to the tumour while sparing nearby organs at risk. In head cases this is unforgiving — targets sit beside optic nerves, the chiasm and the retina, where excess dose can cost a patient their vision.

Different beam types behave very differently here. Could a patient-specific “digital twin” simulate and compare them objectively before any treatment decision?

What I did

Working from anonymised DICOM data for five previously treated patients, I built digital-twin simulations in MatRad (an open-source planning system) and compared four modalities — photons, protons, helium ions and carbon ions.

  • Imported CT and segmented critical structures; configured beam types and incidence angles.
  • Ran dose optimisation, then analysed dose–volume histograms, conformity and homogeneity indices, and NTCP.
  • Evaluated a conventional 5-beam photon plan and matched 2-beam plans across all four modalities for a like-for-like comparison.

Results

Mean dose to critical structures fell sharply as the modality became more conformal. Helium ions gave the best overall balance — sparing the chiasm even better than carbon, thanks to reduced lateral scatter and little nuclear fragmentation — while holding target coverage.

Why it matters

This work laid the first foundations of a predictive digital twin for radiotherapy: a virtual patient that integrates anatomy, imaging and dose to compare strategies and, in the longer term, anticipate toxicity. It also fed a manuscript on comparative NTCP outcomes in digital-twin plans.

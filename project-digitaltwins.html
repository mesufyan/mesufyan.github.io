Summer internship

Digital Twins in Radiotherapy: Beam Comparison & Plan Optimisation

Role: Summer InternWhere: Centre François BaclesseSupervisors: Prof. Juliette Thariat, Dr. Thao Nguyen PhamStack: MatRad, DICOM, DVH

Patient-specific MatRad simulations comparing photon, proton, helium and carbon-ion plans — quantifying which modality best spares vision-critical organs while covering the tumour.

The problem

The central trade-off in radiotherapy is coverage versus collateral: enough dose to the tumour while sparing nearby organs. In head cases this is unforgiving — targets sit beside optic nerves and the chiasm, where excess dose can cost a patient their vision.

Different beam types behave very differently. Could a patient-specific digital twin compare them objectively?

What I did

From anonymised DICOM for five patients, I built digital-twin simulations in MatRad at the Centre François Baclesse and compared four modalities — photons, protons, helium and carbon ions.

  • Imported CT and segmented critical structures; configured beam types and angles.
  • Ran dose optimisation, then analysed DVHs, conformity/homogeneity indices and NTCP.
  • Compared a conventional 5-beam photon plan and matched 2-beam plans across all four modalities.
Schematic of the core idea: photons spread a broad dose wash over the organ at risk, while ions confine dose tightly around the target. (Illustrative — manuscript figures withheld pending publication.)

Results

Mean dose to critical structures fell sharply with more conformal modalities. Helium gave the best overall balance — sparing the chiasm even better than carbon — while holding target coverage.

Why it matters

This laid the first foundations of a predictive digital twin for radiotherapy — a virtual patient integrating anatomy, imaging and dose — and fed a manuscript on comparative NTCP outcomes (currently under review).

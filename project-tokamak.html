Research internship

Plasma Diagnostics at the SMART Tokamak

Role: Research InternWhere: SMART Tokamak, SevilleSupervisor: Prof. Eleonora ViezzerStack: Langmuir probe, I–V analysis

Installing and analysing a Langmuir probe to extract plasma potential, electron temperature and densities from I–V data — validated against the Impedans model.

The setting

SMART is a compact spherical tokamak in Seville. To study a plasma you must first measure it. A Langmuir probe — a small biased conductor — is one of the most widely used tools for this. About SMART.

What I did

I helped install, test and analyse a Langmuir probe, extracted plasma parameters from its current–voltage characteristic, and validated my analysis against the Impedans model.

The Langmuir-probe I–V characteristic: floating potential where current crosses zero, plasma potential at the knee, slopes giving electron temperature.
  • Determined floating and plasma potentials (slope-intersection method).
  • Extracted electron temperature by integrating electron current across the retarding region.
  • Separated ion and electron currents via parameterised cylindrical-probe theory; derived densities.

Results

My pipeline reproduced the instrument’s parameters closely.

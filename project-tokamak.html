Research internship

Plasma Diagnostics at the SMART Tokamak

Role: Research InternWhere: PSFT / SMART Tokamak, SevilleSupervisor: Prof. Eleonora ViezzerStack: Langmuir probe, I–V analysis

Installing and analysing a Langmuir probe to extract plasma potential, electron temperature and densities from I–V data — validated against the Impedans model.

The setting

SMART is a compact spherical tokamak in Seville. To study and control a plasma you first have to measure it — its potentials, temperature and density. A Langmuir probe, a small conductor biased relative to the plasma, is one of the most widely used tools for exactly this.

What I did

I joined the SMART team to install, test and analyse a Langmuir probe, then extracted plasma parameters from its current–voltage (I–V) characteristic and validated my computational analysis against the Impedans reference model.

  • Determined the floating and plasma potentials from the I–V curve (slope-intersection method).
  • Extracted electron temperature by integrating the electron current across the retarding region.
  • Separated ion and electron currents using parameterised cylindrical-probe theory, then derived electron and ion densities.
  • Compared every computed parameter against the instrument’s own output to quantify deviation.

Results

My computational pipeline reproduced the instrument’s parameters closely — confirming the analysis chain works for plasma diagnostics at SMART.

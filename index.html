At the interface of physics, data, and the clinic

I am a graduate researcher at LPC Caen and the Centre François Baclesse, where I develop predictive models to improve dose quality assurance in radiotherapy clinical trials, using machine learning and deep learning on DVH features, RT-DICOM data (CT, RTSTRUCT, RTDOSE) and medical imaging.

I am completing an Erasmus Mundus Joint Master’s in Nuclear Physics across Seville, Catania, and Caen, building on a physics background spanning particle theory, plasma diagnostics, and Monte Carlo dosimetry. My through-line: combining computational methods with problems that have direct clinical impact in cancer care.

PhD direction

Quantify → Integrate → Automate

My thesis lays the foundation for a doctoral project that rethinks radiotherapy QA in three stages.

01 · QUANTIFY

Tumour Topology Complexity

A geometric descriptor (GVC) capturing how intrinsically hard a case is — anatomy plus machine deliverability.

02 · INTEGRATE

A fairer QA verdict

Split a failed plan into “suboptimal planning” vs “complexity-limited”, beyond pass/fail.

03 · AUTOMATE

Multimodal learning

Attention-based Multiple Instance Learning on tabular + DICOM data to predict the new QA classes.

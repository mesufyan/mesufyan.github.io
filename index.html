At the interface of physics, data, and the clinic

I am a graduate researcher at LPC Caen and the Centre François Baclesse, where I develop predictive models to improve dose quality assurance (Dose QA) in radiotherapy clinical trials. My work draws on DVH features, RT-DICOM data (CT, RTSTRUCT, RTDOSE) and medical imaging to make automated QA more consistent, transparent, and fair.

I am completing an Erasmus Mundus Joint Master’s in Nuclear Physics across Seville, Catania, and Caen, building on a physics background that spans particle theory, plasma diagnostics, and Monte Carlo dosimetry. My through-line is the same: combining computational methods with problems that have direct clinical impact in cancer care.

PhD direction

Quantify → Integrate → Automate

My thesis lays the foundation for a doctoral project that rethinks radiotherapy QA in three stages — from a new measure of anatomical complexity to a fully automated, multimodal classifier.

01 · QUANTIFY

Tumour Topology Complexity

A geometric descriptor (GVC) that captures how intrinsically hard a case is to treat — anatomy plus machine deliverability.

02 · INTEGRATE

A fairer QA verdict

Use the descriptor to split a failed plan into “suboptimal planning” vs “complexity-limited”, beyond a blunt pass/fail.

03 · AUTOMATE

Multimodal learning

Attention-based Multiple Instance Learning on tabular + DICOM data to predict the new QA classes automatically.

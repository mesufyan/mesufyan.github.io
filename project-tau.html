BS final-year project

Tau Decays as a Probe of New Physics

Type: BS thesisWhere: COMSATS University IslamabadSupervisor: Prof. Farida TahirStack: FeynArts, FormCalc

Analytical computation of tau leptonic and semi-leptonic decays in the Standard Model and R-parity-violating SUSY, turning lepton-universality tests into bounds on new physics.

The question

The Standard Model assumes electron, muon and tau couple identically to the weak force — lepton universality. Recent anomalies hint this might not be exact, and any genuine violation would signal physics beyond the Standard Model. The heavy, cleanly leptonic tau is an excellent place to look.

Measured lepton-universality g-ratios sit right on the SM prediction of 1 — tight constraints that translate into limits on new physics.

What I did

For my BS thesis under Prof. Farida Tahir, I computed tau decay observables analytically in two frameworks: the Standard Model and R-parity-violating SUSY.

Tree-level tau leptonic decay — the tau emits a W boson, producing a lepton and neutrinos.
  • Derived matrix elements, Feynman amplitudes and decay widths for tau pure-leptonic and semi-leptonic decays.
  • Computed branching ratios and lepton-universality ratios vs experiment.
  • Determined CKM entries (V_ud, V_us) and meson form factors.
  • Placed bounds on R-parity-violating couplings via FeynArts/FormCalc.

Why it still matters to me

This is where I learned to take a precise question, build the full mathematical machinery, and extract quantitative limits — the same rigour I now bring to medical-physics AI.

BS final-year project

Tau Decays as a Probe of New Physics

Type: BS thesisWhere: COMSATS University IslamabadSupervisor: Prof. Farida TahirStack: FeynArts, FormCalc

Analytical computation of tau leptonic and semi-leptonic decays in the Standard Model and R-parity-violating SUSY, turning lepton-universality tests into bounds on new physics.

The question

The Standard Model assumes the three charged leptons — electron, muon, tau — couple identically to the weak force, a property called lepton universality. Recent anomalies hint this might not be exact, and any genuine violation would be a clear signal of physics beyond the Standard Model. The tau, being heavy and cleanly leptonic, is an excellent place to look.

What I did

For my BS thesis I computed tau decay observables analytically in two frameworks: the Standard Model and R-parity-violating Supersymmetry (RPV-SUSY).

  • Derived matrix elements, Feynman amplitudes and decay widths for tau pure-leptonic and semi-leptonic decays.
  • Computed branching ratios and the lepton-universality ratios, comparing predictions with experiment.
  • Determined CKM entries (Vud, Vus) and meson form factors (fπ, fK).
  • Used the universality tests to place bounds on R-parity-violating couplings — i.e. limits on new physics — using FeynArts/FormCalc.

Why it still matters to me

This was where I learned to take a precise scientific question, build the full mathematical machinery, and extract quantitative limits from it. That habit — rigorous formulation, careful computation, honest comparison with data — is exactly what I now bring to medical-physics AI.

Research internship

Toxicity Prediction in Head & Neck Cancer using Machine Learning

Role: Research InternWhere: LPC CaenSupervisors: Prof. Juliette Thariat, Ozgur OzerStack: Python, LightGBM, Optuna, SHAP

A reproducible LightGBM pipeline that predicts xerostomia, mucositis and dysphagia in 684 patients from clinical and dose-volume data — with interpretability built in and limitations stated plainly.

★ View repositoryInternship report

The problem

Modern radiotherapy delivers conformal dose, yet patients still develop toxicities that classical models predict poorly. In head & neck cancer this is acute: targets sit among organs controlling salivation, swallowing and speech.

Can routine clinical variables and DVH-derived dose descriptors predict the risk of xerostomia, mucositis, and dysphagia, patient by patient?

What I did

An end-to-end, reproducible pipeline on a multicentre cohort of 684 patients from the GORTEC network, each toxicity treated as a binary outcome (CTCAE grade 3–5 = severe).

  • Data engineering: linked clinical/treatment/toxicity tables; ordinal-encoded stage; engineered BMI and dose-per-fraction; automated integrity checks.
  • DVH features for organs relevant to each endpoint (parotids for xerostomia, oral cavity for mucositis, PCM/oesophagus for dysphagia).
  • Modelling: LightGBM, 80/20 stratified split, stratified 3-fold CV inside training, Optuna tuning, KNN imputation.
  • Interpretability: SHAP and feature importance to verify clinically plausible drivers.

Results

ROC on the held-out test set. DVH features lifted xerostomia and mucositis clearly above chance.
